As a SNAP recipient, you can also take advantage of the resources below.

Suicide and Crisis Lifeline

Call or Text: 1-844-482-4636

Connects you to trained counselors who provide 24/7, free, and confidential support for anyone experiencing thoughts of suicide or emotional distress.

Crisis Text Line

Text HOME to 741741

Free, 24/7 text-based mental health support from trained crisis counselors.

Grief Support Line

Call 1-800-395-5757

Offers compassionate listening and support for individuals coping with loss and grief.

Self-Harm Hotline

Call 1-800-366-8288

Provides confidential support and resources for those struggling with self-injury or thoughts of self-harm.

TruConnect Lifeline Plan

Free unlimited talk/text/international calling, 4.5GB of data, and a free SIM card.

Safelink Wireless

Free unlimited text and talk. Minutes are capped at 350 free minutes (anything over will be billed). 4.5GB of data, and a free SIM card.

Access from AT&T

Low-cost wireless home internet service to households with at least one resident who participates in the U.S. SNAP program.

Assurance Wireless Cell Phone Plan

Free monthly calls, texts, and data. Free smartphone. $0 cell phone bill. No annual contract. Qualify based on HH income, participation in SSI, SNAP, or Medicaid.

Cricket

$15/month cell phone plans. Includes unlimited talk, text, and 1GB of High-Speed data.

Domestic Violence Hotline

Call 1-800-799-7233

Offers 24-hour confidential support, safety planning, and resources for anyone affected by domestic violence.

Child Protective Services (CPS)

Call 1-877-237-0004

Report suspected child abuse or neglect; available 24/7.

Adult Protective Services

Call 1-888-277-8366

Report abuse, neglect, or exploitation of vulnerable or older adults in Tennessee.

Elder Abuse Hotline

Call 1-800-252-8966

Report elder abuse, neglect, or exploitation; available 24/7.

National Child Abuse Hotline

Call 1-800-422-4453

Provides crisis intervention, information, and resources for children at risk or adults reporting suspected child abuse.

Comcast

Internet service as low as $9.95/month.

Lifeline

A federal program that lowers the monthly cost of phone or internet service. Must be at or below 135% of FPL or receiving SNAP, Medicaid, SSI, Live in public housing, or receiving Veterans Pension and Survivors benefit.

Spectrum

Low-income internet assistance when one or more members of your households are a recipient of one of the following programs: National School Lunch Program (NSLP), Community Eligibility Provision (CEP) for the NSLP, Supplemental Security Income (for applicants age 65+ only)

TN Redline

Call 1-800-889-9789

A 24/7 referral service for Tennesseans seeking information and treatment options for substance use disorders.

Addiction Helpline (SAMHSA National Helpline)

Call 1-800-662-4357

Free, confidential treatment referral and information service for individuals and families facing substance use or mental health issues.

Alcoholics Anonymous (Nashville Area)

Call 1-615-831-1050

Connects callers with local AA meetings and peer support for recovery from alcohol use disorder.

Low Income Home Energy Assistance program

Federally funded grant program that assists low income households in meeting their immediate energy needs. Income eligible households are those at or below 150% of the U.S. Poverty Guidelines.

Weatherization Assistance Program (WAP)

Low-income households includes the installation of weather stripping, caulking, storm windows, insulation, and other materials as well as burner testing, tune-up, and minor repairs.

National HIV/AIDS Hotline

Call 1-800-232-4636

Provides information about HIV testing, prevention, treatment, and local health resources.

Eating Disorder Helpline

Call 1-800-931-2237

Connects callers to trained volunteers who provide information, referrals, and emotional support for eating disorder concerns.

Poison Control

Call 1-800-222-1222

Offers immediate, expert assistance for poison emergencies or exposure questions (available nationwide, 24/7).

Youth Crisis Hotline

Call 1-800-448-4663

Provides 24-hour support for teens and young adults dealing with family conflict, depression, or other crises.

Veterans Crisis Line

Dial 988, then Press 1 or Text 838255

Connects veterans and their families with trained responders for confidential crisis support, available 24/7.

Children’s Health Insurance Program (CHIP)

You may be eligible for the CHIP program if your family’s income is low to moderate and you have a child under age 19 according to United Way.
